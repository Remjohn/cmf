### **1\. The Role of Color in Emotional Storytelling**

Color impacts how viewers **feel** your story.

* **Cool tones** (blue, cyan) \= tension, loneliness, anxiety

* **Warm tones** (orange, gold) \= comfort, joy, intimacy

* **Desaturated looks** \= raw, introspective, real

* **High-contrast looks** \= bold, energetic, intense

For your content, which moves from emotional lows (anxiety, self-doubt) to highs (epiphany, relief), color grading helps visually anchor that transformation.

### **2\. Quick Overview of the Color Wheels (Your Current Understanding is Correct\!)**

In DaVinci Resolve's **Color Page**, the **Color Wheels** section includes:

* **Lift** (Shadows): Adjusts the darkest parts of the image

* **Gamma** (Midtones): Affects skin tones, natural lighting

* **Gain** (Highlights): Alters the brightest areas (sky, reflections)

* **Offset**: Shifts the entire image's tone and color

**Pro Tip:** Hold **Ctrl/Command** while adjusting to slow down your changes for more precision.

### **3\. Your 6–10 Minute Color Grading Workflow**

If you only have a few minutes, this section is key. Here's your step-by-step workflow:

#### **Step 1: Add a LUT (2 minutes)**

* Go to the **Color Page**

* Open the **LUTs** panel (on the left)

* Browse and preview by right-clicking the clip \> "Apply LUT"

* Recommended Free LUT Packs:

  * **Juan Melara's Print Film Emulation LUTs** (subtle, cinematic)

  * **IWLTBAP LUTs** (stylized looks)

  * **Cinematic LUTs for Sony/Canon/GoPro**

Pick one that fits the tone of your video (cool vs warm).

#### **Step 2: Adjust Color Wheels (3 minutes)**

* After applying a LUT, tweak:

  * **Lift**: Slightly lower to add contrast

  * **Gamma**: Raise or lower for skin tone control

  * **Gain**: Boost slightly for brightness or dim to keep moody

#### **Step 3: Correct White Balance (1 minute)**

* Use the **White Balance picker** (eyedropper tool)

* Click something meant to be white/gray in the frame

* Adjust **Temperature** and **Tint** sliders if needed

#### **Step 4: Add Vignette or Focus Window (Optional \- 1 minute)**

* Go to **Window tab** \> Add **Circular window**

* Invert it and reduce exposure outside to guide the eye toward the subject

#### **Step 5: Check Scopes (Optional)**

* Use **Waveform** to make sure your shadows don’t crush and highlights aren’t clipping

### **4\. Tips for Consistency Across Scenes**

To keep a consistent, cinematic look across your micro-story:

* **Group Clips**: Right-click on clips \> "Add into a Group" and grade the Group

* **Still Gallery**: Right-click a graded clip \> Grab Still \> Apply to others

* Use the **Timeline node** (above clip nodes) for global adjustments

---

### **5\. How to Use Free LUTs Efficiently**

#### **Where to Install LUTs:**

1. Go to **Project Settings \> Color Management \> Open LUT Folder**

2. Paste your `.cube` LUT files there

3. Back in Resolve: "Update Lists"

#### **Best Practices:**

* Use LUTs as a **base** only

* Don’t blindly trust them—**always adjust Lift/Gamma/Gain** after applying

* If a LUT is too intense, reduce its strength via **Key Output \> Gain** slider

### **8\. Extra Enhancements to Make it Look Premium**

Even in Resolve Free, you can add these:

* **Sharpening:** Go to **Blur tab** \> reduce radius slightly (makes image crisp)

* **Saturation Boost:** In **Log Wheels**, gently boost midtone saturation

* **Film Grain:** Use overlays (since plugin is Studio-only)

* **Glow (Fusion):** Add soft light accents around highlights manually

### **9\. Save Time: Create Your Own "Look" Template**

After you finish your grade:

* Go to **Gallery**

* Right-click graded clip \> **Grab Still**

* Save this for future projects

* OR: Right-click node tree \> **Save as PowerGrade**

This lets you color match all future reels in seconds.
