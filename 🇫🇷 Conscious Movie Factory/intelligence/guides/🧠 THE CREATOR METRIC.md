## **The Three-Act Structure (Micro Version)**

**Act I:** Present a relatable *problem or desire*  
 **Act II:** The *obvious but wrong* solution fails  
 **Act III:** The *surprising and emotional* right solution works

### **üß© Example:**

## **The Three-Act Structure: A Neurological Framework**

The three-act structure isn't arbitrary‚Äîit mirrors how our brains process problem-solving and learning.

### **Act I: Problem Identification (Setup)**

* **Neurological function**: Pattern recognition and threat assessment  
* **Audience psychology**: Establishing stakes and emotional investment  
* **Surprise opportunity**: Subverting genre expectations or character introductions

### **Act II: Failed Solutions (Confrontation)**

* **Neurological function**: Testing hypotheses and learning from failure  
* **Audience psychology**: Building tension through obstacles  
* **Emotional escalation**: Deepening investment through character struggle

### **Act III: Effective Resolution (Resolution)**

* **Neurological function**: Successful problem-solving and reward  
* **Audience psychology**: Emotional payoff and satisfaction  
* **Surprise delivery**: Unexpected but logical solutions

**Real-World Example \- Die Hard:**

* **Setup**: Ordinary man in extraordinary situation (surprise: action hero who's vulnerable)  
* **Confrontation**: Each escape attempt fails, raising stakes (emotion: increasing desperation)  
* **Resolution**: Uses intelligence over strength (surprise: brain beats brawn)
