## SCENE GENERATION STANDARDS

### Before ANY Scene Prompt:
1. **State the FEELING in one visceral sentence** (not one word)
2. **Describe what THIS person's body does** when feeling this (not generic poses)
3. **Name the specific environment** from their transcript (not assumed spaces)
4. **Cite the timestamp** that grounds this visual

### Banned Visual Elements (Hardcoded Rejections):
*   ‚ùå "Arms open in welcoming gesture"
*   ‚ùå "Direct eye contact with warm smile"
*   ‚ùå "Hand over heart"
*   ‚ùå "Standing in doorway between dark and light"
*   ‚ùå "Walking toward sunrise/light"
*   ‚ùå "Head in hands" (unless transcript-specific)
*   ‚ùå "Silhouette against bright background"
*   ‚ùå Any pose used in a previous project in the same batch

### Visual Contrast Rule:
> **Symbolic elements must POP against the dominant background color.**

When adding any symbolic element to a scene:
1. **Identify the dominant color** of the environment (e.g., green forest, blue hospital, brown earth)
2. **Choose symbolic elements with contrasting colors** (e.g., red/blue bird against green forest)
3. **Avoid camouflage** - a brown bird in brown forest = invisible = wasted symbol

| Environment | Dominant Color | Use Symbol Colors |
|-------------|----------------|-------------------|
| Forest/Garden | Green | Red, Blue, Yellow, White |
| Hospital/Clinical | Blue/White | Red, Orange, Green |
| Urban/Concrete | Gray | Any vivid color |
| Beach/Ocean | Blue | Red, Yellow, Orange |

### Strategic Symbolic Elements Rule:
> **Use FAMILIAR symbols, not obscure ones.**

When adding symbolic elements:
1. **Choose universally recognizable** symbols over regional/niche ones
2. **Parrot > Colibri** - more people recognize a parrot
3. **Butterfly > Moth** - more positive associations
4. **Sunrise > Specific constellation** - universal meaning

Ask: "Would a viewer in Lagos, Paris, AND Tokyo recognize this symbol?"

## üé¨ FINAL PHILOSOPHY

**Your job is not to make "visceral" scenes. Your job is to make SCROLL-STOPPING scenes that HAPPEN to be visceral.**

1. **AESTHETIC** (Would I stop scrolling?)
2. **VISCERAL** (Do I feel something?)
3. **T-CODE COVERAGE** (Did I film the visual anchors?)
4. **TECHNICAL** (Is it well-shot?)

If a visceral technique makes the scene ugly, clinical, or unrelatable‚Äî**it's the wrong technique.**

Use the VP Tier System. Trust the numbers. Focus on TIER S ingredients (VP 8-10) as your foundation, support with TIER A (VP 7-7.5), and completely avoid TIER F (VP < 6).

**Remember: AESTHETIC IS KING. VISCERAL IS THE WEAPON. PRIMAL IS THE FOUNDATION.**
